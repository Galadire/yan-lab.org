About Yan Lab

Yan Lab is dedicated to understanding the molecular mechanisms of mechanosensation, focusing on mechanosensitive ion channels and their roles in sensory processes. Our research spans from structural biology to functional characterization, using a combination of electrophysiology, imaging, and molecular biology approaches.

We are particularly interested in how cells convert mechanical stimuli into electrical signals - a process fundamental to hearing, touch, balance, and many other physiological functions. By studying both vertebrate and invertebrate model systems, we aim to uncover conserved principles and unique adaptations in mechanosensory transduction.

Research Focus

Mechanosensitive Ion Channels

Investigating the structure, function, and regulation of ion channels that respond to mechanical stimuli, including TMC, TMEM63, OSCA, and NompC channels.

  • Channel gating mechanisms
  • Pore properties and ion selectivity
  • Physiological roles in sensory systems

Sensory Transduction

Exploring how mechanical stimuli are converted into electrical signals in various sensory systems, from Drosophila to human models.

  • Auditory transduction mechanisms
  • Touch and pressure sensation
